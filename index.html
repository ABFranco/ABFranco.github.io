CITRIS and the Banatao Institute

Web Developer Intern

During the summer of 2019, I interned as a web developer for the Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society (CITRIS), which is one of UC Berkeley's engineering research centers. Specifically, I worked between CITRIS's communications department and CITRIS's Women in Tech Initiative, a "Research Thrust" in CITRIS.

I had three primary projects during my internship: maintain, improve, and bugfix the main CITRIS website, redesign the WITI@UC website, and continue work on the Innovation Research Database.

Innovation Resource Database

The Innovation Resource Database is a collaborative database that also has API capabilities to access and edit it. Coded with Ruby on Rails back-end and React/JavaScript front-end, my team of 6 utilized Agile practices to efficiently complete user stories and fulfill our client's needs.

This is still an ongoing project, and more info about it can be found on our GitHub page. Its current state as a web app can be found here.

FreeWheel

FreeWheel is an Android application developed in a team of 5 as a class final project. The app had a target audience of users with mobile disabilities, such as wheelchair users, and assisted them to find public transportation methods. I was the primary Front-End programmer as well as implement Google's Maps, Places, and Directions APIs. During the project, we had to conduct user interviews, rapidly prototype, code, and eventually present our project in a public showcase.
